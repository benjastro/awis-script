Reminders:
*If a provider gives you a CPT Code to verify if the service is covered, please state:

"We don't interpret CPT codes but if you can specify what the service is, I can check if the benefits is supported under the member's plan."

The reason we don't interpret CPT codes is because we are NOT medically trained on medical terminology and medical codes. Don't put yourself in a compromising position trying to interpret CPT codes. Just ask the caller to once again specify what the service is for.

*When a provider calls in regarding a specific benefit that is not part of the members core plan we still want to advise of the additional benefits the member might have. This will eliminate the chances of a possible escalation call.
For example:
Provider calls in regarding radiology benefits. (They do not have radiology benefits on their medical plan but they do have an add on that has discounts for radiology.)
We want to advise provider: "They do not have benefits for radiology under their medical plan, however they do have a discount plan that includes radiology. The member can call us directly for us to provide the information of where they need to call/go."

*Provider calls in for pcp visit, (member has telemedicine).
We want to advise provider: "They do not have benefits for an in person pcp visit but they do have telemedicine. This is through a specific company and the member can call us to request additional information."
As always, if a member does not have a specific benefit at all on the account, we want to mention in-network repricing. (If it applies.)

*Remember to advise the provider of any waiting period and pre-existing clause usually mentioned on the bottom part of the brochure.
